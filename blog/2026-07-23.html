Make It Rain(bow)

They say it's the little things that matter most, and what's a garden without a rainbow?

I've created websites many times in the past. Where I fell short was in getting caught in doing everything the right way at the start. I got caught in my own head debating on the best structure and the best technologies to make it right.

This time around, I'm putting things on the page and tweaking as I go. Seeing pages with content gives me a much better idea of what it could look like. I can see where I'm repeating code to focus on refactoring strategies with biggest impact.

I'm planting seeds, pulling weeds, watering the garden (you can tell, because of the rainbow). Not trying to build an elaborate fully grown tree from the ground up. I'm going to need a gardening analogies page.

Each little change I make makes this look more like a website and less like a text document opened in a browser. This is where the real dopamine hit happens.
